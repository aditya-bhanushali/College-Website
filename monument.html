Taj Mahal
Located in Agra, the Taj Mahal is an iconic symbol of love and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture and intricate designs.
Qutub Minar
Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world. Built in the 12th century, it is a testament to Indo-Islamic architecture and history.
Gateway of India
The Gateway of India, located in Mumbai, is a historic arch-monument built in the early 20th century. Overlooking the Arabian Sea, it symbolizes the grandeur of British colonial architecture.
Hawa Mahal
Located in Jaipur, Hawa Mahal, or the "Palace of Winds," is a pink sandstone structure built in 1799. It features 953 intricately designed windows that allowed royal women to observe street life without being seen.
